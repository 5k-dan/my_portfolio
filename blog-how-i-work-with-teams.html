How I Work with Teams

When working in a team, I find open conversation very important. I make sure that everyone gets a chance to bring out their thoughts and ideas, which often leads to new and creative solutions.

For example, in the previous hackathon, my colleagues and I were able to make a chatbot using each of our skills: some worked on the front part, and others on the AI model. As everyone was clear about task sharing and communication during the project, we ended up creating a much better product than expected.

During my first week of college, I had the opportunity to join the Multicultural Engineering Program. One of our activities involved working in a team to draft a solution to the Exxon Valdez oil spill of 1989. This project was both challenging and inspiring, as we brainstormed innovative methods for oil spill containment and cleanup.

My team worked tirelessly to research the environmental and economic impact of the spill, ultimately presenting a detailed plan that incorporated both technical and community-based solutions. This experience taught me the value of diverse perspectives in solving real-world problems and highlighted the power of collaboration in achieving meaningful results.

I will also mention feedback and flexibility. With groups, I seek from others constructive criticism that helps me deliver improved results. I really believe in celebrating wins together and learning from difficulties as a team. Such ideas have helped me form solid, long-lasting bonds with my team members and deliver excellent project results every time.
